Deployable Space Structures
I am currently researching new designs for deployable space structures. Deployability is useful because it enables structures to fit into small volumes in order to be launched into space, where they then unfold for various purposes. Examples include solar arrays, shape-changing robots, and structures for habitats on other planetary bodies.
Laser Melting of Lunar Regolith Simulants
I investigated how the particle size of lunar regolith simulants affects the properties of laser-melted products. Laser melting of lunar regolith can be used for construction on the Moon, and is beneficial because it requires very few resources to be brought from Earth to the Moon. Seiving the lunar regolith to separate the different sized particles before laser melting can improve many of the properties of the melted products.

Lunar Regolith Simulant Development
I worked on the development of high-fidelity lunar regolith simulants at TU Berlin. Often, lunar regolith simulants are used in applications ranging from astronaut training to lunar construction research, because of the limited quantity of true lunar regolith available on Earth. High-fidelity lunar regolith simulants are designed to mimic the chemical, mineralogical, and physical properties of the lunar regolith at any location on the lunar surface.
